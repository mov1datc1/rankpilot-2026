…leen disputes de forma distinta.
Y eso es oro para RankPilot.

🔥 PRINCIPIO FUNDAMENTAL
El mismo matter puede ser:
🔥 excelente para Chambers
😐 mediocre para Legal 500
🚫 irrelevante para IFLR
⭐ muy fuerte para Leaders League

Entonces:
la RAG debe tener una capa:
Ranking Body Logic
Es decir:
IF guide = Chambers
→ apply Chambers weighting

IF guide = Legal 500
→ apply Legal 500 logic

IF guide = IFLR
→ prioritize finance-linked disputes

IF guide = Leaders League
→ prioritize visibility / market perception / sector reputation

🧠 DISPUTES RAG — LEGAL 500
Diferencia clave vs Chambers
Legal 500 suele ser:
más narrativa
más inclusiva
menos rígida en taxonomía
más receptiva a “full service disputes”
más abierta a mixed submissions
Mientras Chambers:
segmenta más agresivamente
penaliza category mismatch más fuerte

Entonces el RAG Legal 500 debe:
Ser MENOS excluyente
Ejemplo:
Chambers:
Arbitration in Commercial Litigation = weighting risk
Legal 500:
Arbitration may still reinforce broader disputes capability

Legal 500 privilegia más:
🟢 Breadth of practice
litigation
arbitration
investigations
mediation
cross-border disputes
…pueden convivir mejor.

Legal 500 privilegia:
🟢 Client relationship depth
🟢 Team depth
🟢 Responsiveness
🟢 Commerciality
🟢 Sector integration
Mucho más que Chambers.

Entonces el RAG debe detectar:
- client intimacy
- cross-practice collaboration
- long-term client mandates
- responsiveness indicators
- practical/commercial approach

Legal 500 — lógica disputes
Más flexible:
IF dispute work is commercially coherent
→ may still strengthen general disputes positioning

🚨 Pero igual necesita exclusiones
Ejemplo:
Pure employment litigation
→ should still go to Employment

🧠 DISPUTES RAG — LEADERS LEAGUE
Aquí cambia TODO.
Porque Leaders League es muchísimo más:
🟣 Market perception driven

Privilegian:
visibility
prestige
market notoriety
major mandates
sector dominance
international footprint
reputation among peers
Menos:
taxonomía hiper estricta

Entonces el RAG debe detectar:
🟢 Market signals
high-profile disputes
media visibility
strategic clients
landmark mandates
sector leadership
repeat institutional clients
regional influence

Leaders League tolera mucho más:
mixed disputes portfolios

Entonces:
Chambers pregunta:
Does this belong here?
Leaders League pregunta:
Does this firm dominate this market?

🔥 Esto es ENORME
Porque significa que:
un mismo submission debe evaluarse distinto.

Ejemplo REAL
Mixed disputes practice:
arbitration
litigation
investigations
civil fraud
banking disputes

Chambers:
⚠️ “too diffuse”

Legal 500:
🟢 “full-service disputes capability”

Leaders League:
🟢 “market-leading disputes platform”

🧠 DISPUTES RAG — IFLR
Aquí el cambio es MÁS radical aún.
Porque IFLR NO es realmente “general disputes”.
IFLR vive alrededor de:
🔵 Financial & corporate transactional sophistication

Entonces disputes solo importa cuando toca:
banking
restructuring
capital markets
financial institutions
insolvency
shareholder/corporate governance
regulatory finance
project finance disputes
financial fraud exposure

Entonces:
Un commercial dispute normal:
🚫 probablemente irrelevante para IFLR

Pero esto:
restructuring litigation
shareholder governance dispute
banking enforcement
bondholder litigation
financial fraud
insolvency disputes
→ sí puede importar muchísimo.

Entonces el RAG IFLR debe:
Penalizar:
general commercial litigation
claimant work
consumer disputes
ordinary litigation
employment disputes
local court disputes

Y privilegiar:
🟢 Financial complexity
structured finance disputes
restructuring conflicts
banking litigation
securities disputes
distressed asset disputes
regulatory banking exposure
insolvency proceedings
cross-border financial enforcement

🔥 ENTONCES:
La misma disputa cambia completamente según la guía

Ejemplo
Shareholder dispute after failed acquisition
Chambers:
🟢 Commercial Litigation
Legal 500:
🟢 Commercial Disputes
Leaders League:
🟢 High-profile corporate dispute
IFLR:
🟢 ONLY if linked to governance / capital markets / financial structure
